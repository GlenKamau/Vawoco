Our Core Focus Areas

We work with business & provide solution to client with their business problem

E-Waste Project

Our E-waste initiative is built on the C2R model: Collect, Repair, Resell. We collect used and damaged smartphones, refurbish them, and offer them at affordable prices to underserved communities.

Upcycling Project

In our upcycling project, we collect old clothes and transform them into stylish, functional products. These items are sold at affordable prices, especially targeting low-resource communities.

Skills Training and Certification

Our third focus area is skills development through training and certification programs. We empower youth and vulnerable women with practical skills in mobile phone repair and tailoring, enabling them to earn a livelihood and become self-reliant

Our Projects

We provide a wide range of creative services

Women’s Empowerment Circles.

We organize community-based support groups where women receive training in financial literacy, leadership, and entrepreneurship. Through mentorship and shared experiences, members grow both personally and economically, gaining confidence and self-reliance

Youth Skills for Life

A practical training initiative equipping youth with life skills and market-relevant trades—such as tailoring, agribusiness, ICT, and carpentry. Our goal is to reduce unemployment by connecting training with real-world income opportunities.

Community Health Outreach.

In partnership with local health workers, we provide free maternal and child healthcare clinics, HIV/AIDS awareness campaigns, and sanitary product distribution. These efforts reduce preventable diseases and promote healthier households.

Back-to-School for Vulnerable Children

This project identifies children from marginalized backgrounds and supports them with school uniforms, fees, supplies, and psychosocial support—giving them a fair chance at education.

Water for Wellness

VAWOCO supports the construction of boreholes and water tanks in arid regions. By improving water access, communities reduce waterborne diseases and empower girls who no longer need to walk long distances for water.

Green Futures: Women in Farming

This program trains women in sustainable agriculture, kitchen gardening, and agri-business. We promote organic practices and connect farmers with markets to increase food security and household income.
